How to Assess Your Japanese Level

Choosing the right class level is crucial for a positive and effective learning experience. Hereâ€™s how you can determine the best fit for you:

1. Self-Assessment based on JLPT Levels

Our class levels are generally aligned with the Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT). Review the descriptions on our Class Levels page.

  • N5 (Beginner): Can understand some basic Japanese. Recognizes hiragana, katakana, and basic kanji.
  • N4 (Elementary): Can understand basic Japanese used in everyday situations.
  • N3 (Intermediate): Can understand Japanese used in everyday situations to a certain degree.
  • N2 (Upper-Intermediate): Can understand Japanese used in everyday situations and in a variety of circumstances to a certain degree.
  • N1 (Advanced): Can understand Japanese used in a broad range of circumstances.

2. Review Your Previous Study

Consider the following:

  • Textbooks Used: Textbooks like Genki I & II, Minna no Nihongo I & II, Tobira, etc., often correspond to specific levels.
    • Genki I completion is roughly N5.
    • Genki II completion is roughly N4.
    • Intermediate textbooks (e.g., Tobira, An Integrated Approach to Intermediate Japanese) often target N3-N2.
  • Hours of Study: While highly variable, a rough guideline:
    • N5: ~150-250 hours
    • N4: ~300-500 hours
    • N3: ~450-700 hours
    • N2: ~600-1000 hours
    • N1: ~900-1500+ hours
  • Time Spent in Japan / Using Japanese: Practical experience can significantly impact your conversational ability.

3. Online Placement Tests (General Guidance)

Several websites offer free Japanese placement tests. While not definitive, they can provide a general idea of your level. Search for "Japanese language placement test" online. (Note: Kyoto University does not endorse specific third-party tests for official placement).

4. Consultation

If you are still unsure, please indicate "Unsure / Need Assessment" on the sign-up form, or contact us. We may ask you a few questions or arrange a brief informal chat to help determine the most suitable class for you.

During the first week of classes, instructors will also assess if students are in the appropriate level and can suggest a transfer if necessary.

Our goal is to place you in a class where you will be challenged but not overwhelmed, ensuring a productive and enjoyable learning experience.
